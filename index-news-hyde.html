What is a penumbral eclipse of the moon?

Wednesday, 06 November 2013 14:06

A penumbral eclipse is more subtle, and more difficult to observe, than a total or partial eclipse. It happens when the moon moves through the outer part of Earth’s shadow.

June Moore (1919-2013)

Tuesday, 05 November 2013 19:14

June Moore was the wife of Carroll L. Moore longtime Nebraska Wesleyan professor of astronomy and geology. Neither Prairie Astronomy Club or Hyde Observatory would exist except for the efforts of Carroll and June. June was a long-time community volunteer. After Carroll passed, she funded the white bench in front of Hyde Observatory dedicated in Carroll’s memory.
